Nearby Attractions

Explore the wonders of the Tasman Peninsula

Welcome to the Tasman Peninsula

Four Seasons Waterfront Villas is perfectly positioned to explore some of Australia's most spectacular scenery and historic sites. The Tasman Peninsula is rich with things to see and do — from ancient convict history to dramatic coastal landscapes.

Not sure where to start? Contact us and we'll be happy to point you in the right direction!

Historic Sites

Port Arthur Historic Site

One of Australia's most significant convict sites and UNESCO World Heritage listed. A must-see on the Tasman Peninsula, just 13km away.

Visit website →

Eaglehawk Neck

The narrow land bridge that kept convicts on the Port Arthur peninsula. Home to dramatic coastal formations and a fascinating military history.

Learn more →

Natural Wonders

Pirate's Bay Lookout

Breathtaking panoramic views over Pirate's Bay and the rugged coastline. A short walk rewards you with some of the best scenery on the peninsula.

Tessellated Pavement

A fascinating natural geological formation on the foreshore at Eaglehawk Neck. The flat rock slabs look like they've been tiled by hand.

Learn more →

Three Capes Track

Australia's premier multi-day coastal walk. The 48km track takes you to Cape Hauy, Cape Pillar and Cape Raoul — featuring the world's highest sea cliffs.

Visit website →

Cape Hauy

A rewarding day walk with dramatic dolerite columns and spectacular ocean views. One of Tasmania's most iconic coastal destinations.

Cape Raoul

A challenging but stunning walk through coastal heath and forest to sheer cliff tops with incredible views across Storm Bay.

Wine and Fine Food

Bangor Vineyard Shed

A working sheep and cattle station with an impressive wine and cheese offering. Spectacular views over Dunalley Bay.

Visit website →

1830 Restaurant and Bar

Located at the Port Arthur Historic Site, 1830 offers seasonal Tasmanian produce in a stunning heritage setting.

Visit website →

Fox & Hounds Inn

A cosy pub and restaurant in Copping serving hearty meals. A local favourite for a relaxed lunch or dinner.

Port Arthur Lavender Farm

Stroll through rows of lavender and sample lavender-infused products at this charming working farm.

McHenry Distillery

Tasmania's southernmost distillery, producing award-winning craft spirits including gin and whisky. Tours and tastings available.

Visit website →

Fishing and Diving

EagleHawk Dive Centre

World-class diving in the crystal-clear waters of the Tasman Peninsula. Explore kelp forests, caves, and abundant marine life.

Visit website →

Tasman Spirit Charters

Fishing and wildlife cruises on the Tasman Peninsula. Spot dolphins, seals and seabirds while chasing the big catch.
